❔ How this works

This tool turns the invoices your performers email in into the files you need to pay them correctly and stay compliant with the new contractor-super rules. There are two halves: running the pay run, then exporting the files.

① Running a pay run — 3 steps

  1. Refresh from Zoho. Pulls the current contractors and confirmed bookings (plus super-fund details) so everything matches live data. Start every pay run here.
  2. Enter invoice data. Drag in the performers' PDF invoices (or pull them from Gmail). The tool reads each one, matches it to a Zoho contractor, and works out the GST and super treatment.
  3. Review & export. Check each invoice in the review screen — confirm the amount, the matched booking, and whether to deduct super — then download the export files.

② Exporting the files — 3 uploads, in order

  1. Xero contractor bills → import to Xero. Pays each performer their net amount.
  2. Xero super bills → import to Xero. Bills the super to your clearing house (paid as a separate batch).
  3. AustralianSuper SAFF → upload to AustralianSuper's Employer Portal. Sends the super contributions into each performer's fund.

(The invoice-PDF ZIP is an optional extra — renamed PDFs you can drag onto the matching Xero bills.)

Contractor types

Each contractor is one of four types, which decides GST and super:

  • A — individual / sole trader, no GST → super applies.
  • B — individual / sole trader, registered for GST → super applies.
  • C — company or partnership, no GST → no super.
  • D — company or partnership, registered for GST → no super.

What "deduct super" means

For individual performers (types A & B), MEC must pay super on their fee from 1 July 2026. "Deduct super" sets aside 12% (or the contractor's Zoho rate): the performer is paid their fee net on the contractor bill, and the super amount is billed separately to your clearing house and sent to their fund via the SAFF file. Your total cost is the same — it's just split between cash to the performer and super to their fund. Companies/partnerships (C & D) have no super deducted.

📊 Xero Bills — Contractor

Bill 1. One bill per contractor engagement, paid net (invoice total minus super). The super amount is noted on the first line; there is no separate super line for non-GST contractors. Import via Accounting → Purchases → Import and pay in the normal ABA batch.

Export 2 of 3 · uploads to Xero

🧾 Xero Bills — Super

Bill 2. One bill per super-eligible invoice, billed to the clearing-house supplier, single description line, coded to 478-C (BAS Excluded). Import alongside the contractor bills but pay as a separate ABA batch.

Export 3 of 3 · uploads to AustralianSuper

🏦 AustralianSuper SAFF (SuperStream)

The SuperStream Alternative File Format file you upload to AustralianSuper's Employer Portal / QuickSuper (Contribution Files → Upload File). Built to the QuickSuper Contribution CSV spec (columns A–AM); one row per super-eligible performer.
